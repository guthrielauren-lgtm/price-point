Quick Heads Up
HERE'S WHAT YOU'RE
ABOUT TO LEARN.

You're a junior merchant at VELD Outerwear. You'll make six pricing calls across three rounds — each one adding a new wrinkle. Some decisions will feel obvious. Some will surprise you. All of them will teach you something.

The Big Ideas
1
Brand tier is price tier
Consumer price expectations are set by who you are — before a single product detail is considered.
2
Competitors are guardrails, not targets
Know where they price. Price where VELD belongs. Anchoring to competitors is a trap.
3
Positioning and margin have to work together
The right price holds up emotionally for the consumer and financially for the business. Both, at the same time.
4
Markdowns are a last resort
Reactive price cuts erode brand positioning and train consumers to wait. Hold unless there's a real reason not to.

The Origin
BUILT FOR PEOPLE WHO
MOVE THROUGH HARD
PLACES QUIETLY.

Erik and Maren Solberg spent a decade designing technical gear for European military contracts before relocating from Oslo to Bend, Oregon in 2009. They were tired of performance apparel that looked like it belonged in a lab — gear that communicated capability through logos and colorways instead of construction.

VELD's first product was a single hardshell jacket — made in a 600 sq ft workshop, sold exclusively through three independent retailers in the Pacific Northwest. The name comes from the Afrikaans word for open wilderness. The philosophy hasn't moved since.

"We don't make gear for the gram. We make gear for the moment after the photo — when it's cold, you're tired, and you need it to work."

— Erik Solberg, Co-Founder

Your Position
WHERE VELD LIVES.

In every scenario, you'll see two competitors — one above VELD, one below. That gap is intentional. VELD is not chasing the ultra-premium brand. VELD is not discounting to the heritage brand. VELD owns the space in between.
